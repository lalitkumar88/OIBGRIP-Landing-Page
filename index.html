Public Reviews

A.A. Shitole, Founder and CEO, TTMM

There are thousands of such heritage sites in India, whose structure is very strong. Many forts of Rajasthan have been converted into hotels equipped with modern facilities. In addition to being used as hotels or museums or libraries, buildings of this type can easily be adapted to be used as schools or clinics. The first step in this direction would be to ensure that visits to monuments and archaeological sites prove to be an exciting experience for the visitors. After decades of archaeological efforts, thousands of heritage sites have been discovered in India that are contemporary to the famous Harappan civilization. Very few people know about these places, while the tourism department has also failed to publicize the historical importance of these places, immediate effective steps will have to be taken in this direction. India has 35 UNESCO-recognized World Heritage Sites, only a few countries in the world have more than 35.It is necessary to collect information about the music, food, rituals, dress, personality, sports, festivals etc. associated with these places so that we can make economic profit by giving these information to the visitors. The hardwork done by Sahyadri Foundation in Maharashtra is really nice, the nation now needs the NGO's like Sahyadri Foundation. Best luck for your future endeavours.

P.R. Suryawanshi, President, Tours&Travells Organization of India

Various types of tasks done by Sahyadri Foundation is admirable. Continuous efforts for preserving the natural beauty of our state and hence this world is admirable. It would be unfortunate to say that till now we have been talking only on the social and cultural importance of our heritage sites, while their role in economic development is very important. If we want to take forward our heritage, then it has to be linked to economic benefits and for this, the cultural sector should be brought under the purview of liberalization, when these places become of economic importance, private and public sectors will be involved in their maintenance. This is one aspect that we have been ignoring.Today I, on behalf of our organization declare full support to Sahyadri Foundation.

G.R. Jagtap, National Tourism Adviser

The relation of a person to his heritage is like that of a child to his mother. Our heritage is our pride and it strengthens our sense of history. India has been a country whose history is full of diversity, from the Harappan civilization, the oldest in the world, where we can learn the best city-planning tricks, to the Jantar Mantar and observatories of Jaipur, we can see the glimpse of contemporary scientific development. The foundation stones of our art and culture are also our heritage sites.Tourism is the main vehicle of national development. Tourism is going to play an important role in making the ambitious Make in India initiative of the Government of India successful. To create employment for lakhs of young Indians, the country will have to grow at the rate of 9-10 percent continuously for a few decades and to achieve this we will have to take advantage of our rich tourism resources. Sahyadri Foundation is a prominent organization in India which is dedicated to protection of forts in the state of Maharashtra. Group tasks done by them is really admirable and I wish a very best luck for thier future activities.

About Us

We, the Sahyadri Foundation are the promint organization working majorly in the Indian state of Maharashtra. We have successfully completed more than 750 fort protection campaign till date and going strong. Our working region is not only Maharashtra but all over India. We are very much concern about our national cultural heritage and we will protect it from wrong interference, and that is what our main target is. We are also looking for active participation of people and for that reason we falicitate our best members with awards like Shivdurg Puraskar, Shivrath Puraskar, Asmita Puraskar, we also arrange walking campaign on the occasion birth anniversary of Chh. Sambhaji Maharaj from Bhakti-Shakti Garden Nigdi,Pune to fort Purandar(which is birth place of Chh. Sambhaji Maharaj). Our campaign primarily a example of brotherhood as the people of all religions and casts participates in the campaigns and this shows national integrity. This foundation completely work on donation from the members. Money is primarily used to reconsrtuct or renovation of forts. A fixed amount at the end of every year is donated to various NGO's for better education, better health, better environment. We also arrange sessions of some famous lecturers like Prof.Nitin Bangude Patil, Prof.Vishal Garad occasionaly which is proving worthy for our members
